❓How to

How to use What If Studeos

Idea → script → finished vertical video → posted. The full loop, step by step.

1Start everything with one click

Start-What-If-Studio.bat 🎛 Dashboard 👁 Watcher ✦ Studio opens

One double-click starts the whole studio — safe to click again anytime.

  • Open the project folder and double-click Start-What-If-Studio.bat.
  • It starts the dashboard (Videos + Produce pages + the AI writer), the watcher (auto-renders your exports), and opens the Studio in your browser.
  • Tip: right-click the file → Send to → Desktop for a one-click desktop shortcut.

2Pick an idea — or write your own

🎬 Generate + Export

Every scenario becomes a complete production package: hooks, script beats, shot list, captions, thumbnail text.

  • Click any scenario card in the library (search or filter by category). Besides the eight "what if" categories there are two story categories that brand their own videos automatically — no extra settings: Scary Story (narrative horror told straight: trapped divers, night-shift rules, the 3:33 doorbell, the open house that never ends) gets eerie social-thriller visuals — still, symmetrical, quietly-wrong frames — a "follow for more scary stories" outro and end card, and horror hashtags; True History (real documented events: the dancing plague, the Emu War, the bear who served in the army) gets archival AI visuals, a "follow for more true history" outro and end card, and history hashtags. Each story category also has its own title-card and cover typography — stark, thin, widely-spaced art-house caps for Scary Story (the horror-poster look), an aged 17th-century serif for True History — while the spoken captions stay in the standard font for readability. The music bed follows too: a shelf of creepy beds under Scary Story — drones, music boxes, dark choirs, ghost-story pianos — mixed a notch louder than other categories, and period lute-and-strings under True History (credit lines land in the post kit as always). Scary Story videos also get beat-timed sound design: a sub-bass riser swells in the seconds before the reveal beat and lands with a soft impact on its first word, timed from the same word boundaries as the captions.
  • Your own idea? + Create your own scenario → type just the title → ✨ Write it for me drafts the premise and beats (everything stays editable). Pick the video length right next to the button ("for a 90s video") — longer runtimes get longer beats, which means more narration and a longer video. The choice syncs with Package Settings both ways. With an OpenAI key in pipeline\openai_key.txt the draft comes from OpenAI in a couple of seconds; without one it uses the free writer.
  • Choose runtime and voice style — the voice style picks the narrator (Calm=Adam, High-Energy=Callum, Deadpan=George with ElevenLabs). No platform toggle: one cut works on TikTok, Shorts, and Reels, and each finished video's post kit carries the right hashtags for all three.

✦Or just tell the assistant

The ✦ button in the bottom-right corner — on every page, this one included — opens a chat assistant that knows the whole app.

  • On the Studio page it can drive everything: “make a 90s deadpan video about haunted elevators” drafts a new scenario, adds it to your library, and opens it — add “and render it” and it exports for the watcher too. It knows the story categories: “make a scary story about the night shift” or “make a true history video about the emu war” lands in the right category with a plain narrative title (no “What if” wrapper). It also handles the small stuff: “open the black hole scenario”, “set the voice to high-energy”, “make it 3 min”, “generate”, “export srt”, “give me an idea”.
  • On every page it answers questions — how rendering works, where keys go, what the runtimes mean — checks on your renders (“is my video done?” shows render progress and your newest finished videos), hands you post kits (“give me the caption for my newest video” fetches the caption + hashtags with a copy button), tracks what went out (“mark it posted” flags the video, same as the Videos page toggle), and jumps around for you: “go to studio”, “go to produce”, “go to videos”. The conversation follows you from page to page (in the same tab), so “go to videos … now mark it posted” just works.
  • Commands and app questions work fully offline; with the Studio helper running (the badge says AI online) it also answers free-form questions and figures out fuzzier requests, using OpenAI when a key is set or the free writer otherwise.
  • It never touches anything destructive — deleting scenarios and “Reset all local data” stay manual, and nothing is ever posted anywhere.

3One click — the video makes itself

🎬 Generate + Export 📄 📁 Rendering 1 video…

The watcher spots your export and renders it automatically — free AI images, voice, captions, music, covers.

  • Click 🎬 Generate + Export for render — it builds the package and downloads the render .json in one step. The watcher takes it from there.
  • Render a whole category at once: pick a category filter in the library and a 🎬 Render all N … button appears — one click exports every scenario in it as a single queue, and the watcher renders them back-to-back (a few minutes each, free, using your current runtime and voice). Or just tell the assistant: “render all scary stories”.
  • Skim the Script Beats tab while it renders — that's exactly what the voice says. Edited something? Export .json (render again) sends a new cut.
  • A few minutes later the output folder opens with the finished video. New scenarios take 2–5 minutes each (AI images generate once, then they're cached).

4Premium videos — the Produce page

Copy prompt tryinfer Studio ⬇⬇⬇ Import from Downloads ✓ Done

Real AI motion video per beat, using your unlimited tryinfer Studio subscription — no renaming, no folder hunting. With an OpenAI key in pipeline\openai_key.txt, every prompt is automatically rewritten into a richer cinematic direction (once per scenario, then cached).

  • Sidebar → 🎞️ Produce → pick your package from the dropdown. The dropdown is your export history: every export is archived permanently (pipeline\exports), listed newest-first with its date and how many clips you've staged for it — nothing gets overwritten anymore.
  • 🌅 Morning batch: while the studio is running, the dashboard drafts 3 fresh 90s Scary Story packages every morning (after 6:00) all by itself — they appear at the top of the package dropdown, prompts ready to copy into tryinfer Studio whenever you have a break. Want more right now? Two buttons under the dropdown draft on demand: 🌅 Draft 3 fresh scary scenarios and 💭 Draft 3 realistic what-ifs — the what-ifs are grounded thought experiments built from real science, economics, or everyday life (never monsters or magic), matching the plausible tone of your existing videos. The Clips dropdown next to the buttons picks how many clips each drafted video uses — 7 (classic), 9, 12, or 15 (hook + story beats + outro; remembered for next time). More clips = faster cuts, same video length: the writer splits the same story across shorter spoken lines, every clip still lands on its own beat, and the reveal (with its riser + impact sound) still hits on the second-to-last one. At 15 clips each beat runs ~6s, so a 5s tryinfer clip covers it nearly wall-to-wall. It drafts only — nothing renders, spends tryinfer credit, or posts without you.
  • 🎯 Workflow picker (top of step 2): choose what you're making — 🎞️ Modern trailer, 🎙 Narrated trailer, 🎵 Ironic horror, or 📖 Classic — and the page sets itself up: the right 🎭 mood, the right music checkbox, a suggested Clips count, and a numbered ①②③ trail appears on the buttons to follow in order (irrelevant options dim). Picking a preset cascades once; reloading never overwrites your tweaks. Free-form turns the guidance off.
  • Spot a typo or want a pause? Open ✏️ Script, fix the title/hook/beats/outro right there (add a — or … where the narrator should breathe) → Save script → render. The title is also the video's cover text and updates the package dropdown; the voice, captions, and post kit all follow; your staged clips and caches stay connected.
  • Pick your visuals in step 2: ⚡ AI video (tryinfer API — one real motion clip per beat; pick the model: seedance-2.0-pro is the premium default at ≈$0.65 per 5s clip, while fast/flash models like seedance-2.0-fast or wan2.2-flash cost far less. tryinfer doesn't publish rates, so each model's price appears in the dropdown after your first render with it — learned from your own billed charges via the Spend ledger; cached clips are free), 🖼️ AI images (one styled picture per beat with gentle Ken Burns motion — engine Free Pollinations or any tryinfer image model like nano-banana or flux-2, billed per image; choose Cinematic, 3D, Infographic, Dark, Eerie, or Archival — the style pre-picks itself from the package's category when you select one (Eerie for Scary Story, Archival for True History, Cinematic otherwise), and you can always override it), or 🎬 My own clips.
  • Manual path (uses your Studio sub instead of credit): Copy each numbered prompt → paste into tryinfer Studio (9:16 · 5s · your model) → generate → download in order → ⬇ Import newest N videos from Downloads. Each clip lands on its beat (oldest first = generation order) with the radio set to 🎬 video — you see every clip next to the line it plays under, hover to preview, and fix a mismatch by re-dropping a file on the right row. Dropping several files on a row fills consecutive beats.
  • Don't like a prompt? Rewrite it. Click any numbered prompt, type your own scene, then Save prompts — the Copy buttons and every AI mode (video and images) use your wording from then on, and the style tail (cinematic, 9:16…) is re-added automatically so you never have to type it. Editing the script resets the prompts to match the new beats. Each row also shows the 🎙 spoken line (hook / beat / outro) the narrator says over that clip, so you always know which words go with which visual — and it's editable right there: click the line, type, then ✓ save (or ↩ undo). Click ✨ and the AI rewrites it in the tone you pick from the dropdown next to it — Punchy, 😂 Funny, 🌍 Nat Geo documentary, 😏 Tongue-in-cheek, 🎭 Satire, 🎬 Dramatic, or 🧒 Simple (same facts, same length; the choice applies to every row and is remembered) — and when that beat has a selected image or video, the AI looks at it and rewrites the line to match what's on screen (for videos it reads the first frame). ✓ keep writes it into the script (voice, captions, and post kit follow; your visual prompts are kept), ↩ undo throws it away. Uses your OpenAI key when present, otherwise the free writer (text-only).
  • Want more (or fewer) than 7 clips? Every row has a ＋ beat button (insert a new beat right after it — write its line and prompt, or attach media and let the AI describe it) and body beats have 🗑 beat (delete it; the spoken line and prompt stay in history). Attached images/videos shift along with their beats automatically. The video is exactly as long as the narration, so more beats = a longer video; minimum 3 body beats, up to 20 rows total.
  • Nothing saved is ever lost. Every beat row has its own 🕘 History button: past versions of that beat's spoken line and prompt, with Use to bring one back (it lands as a proposal — keep / Save prompts to apply). The 🕘 Full history panel below the prompts lists whole saved versions of the script and prompts (the very first save also archives the original), and Restore brings any of them back — restoring is recorded too, so you can even revert a revert. Note the flip side: an auto-described prompt or ✨ suggestion you haven't saved/kept disappears on refresh — images and videos, by contrast, are always saved the moment you attach them.
  • Or start from a picture — or your own clip. Every prompt row has an ＋ image and an ＋ video box (also: drag a file onto the row, or point at it and paste Ctrl+V for pictures). Both are saved with the package (click to replace, ✕ to remove), and the radio button next to them picks which one the beat uses. Attaching an image auto-describes it into the prompt (so frame and motion always agree — undo or retype if you prefer your own words); 🖼 From image re-runs it anytime, and 🎬 From video does the same from your clip's first frame. All need the OpenAI key; each costs a fraction of a cent. Tweak, then Save prompts.
  • Radio on the image: the picture ends up in the video, whatever the mode. In ⚡ AI video mode that beat is animated from your picture — cropped to 9:16 and sent as the clip's first frame, so the video literally starts on your photo (beats without one keep the auto-generated first frame); swap the image and only that beat regenerates on the next render, the rest stay cached and free. In every other mode (my own clips, 🖼️ AI images, stock) the picture itself is that beat's visual, shown full-frame with the same gentle camera move the AI images get — so you can build a whole video from nothing but attached pictures and hit 🎬 Render.
  • 🎭 Mood + the two rewrite buttons (above the prompt rows): pick a mood — Eerie, Funny, Sarcastic, Witty, Adventurous, Dramatic, Mysterious, Wholesome, Inspiring, or Deadpan (remembered) — then 📝 Script from prompts AI-writes the whole spoken script (hook, beats, outro) fitted to the visual prompts in that mood (the prompts stay exactly as they are — great after you've built the shot list first, or imported clips and described them), while 🎬 Prompts from script goes the other way and re-imagines every visual prompt from the spoken lines, the mood expressed through lighting, framing, and body language. Both save immediately and both leave the previous version one click away in 🕘 History. Word budgets follow the runtime and clip count, so the video length doesn't drift. The same mood rides along with every ✨ line rewrite, and — when it's set to anything other than 🎯 Auto — it's folded into the style of generated visuals at render time too (AI images, AI-video frames and motion), so one dropdown styles words and pictures together. Auto = each category's own register, visuals exactly as before. Generated-visual caches aren't forked by mood: delete a scenario's cache entry (pipeline\ai-visuals or pipeline\infer-images) to restyle already-rendered beats.
  • 🎵 Ironic cheerful music (a Render checkbox): the Jordan Peele trick — a sincerely happy song that contradicts the visuals. The render picks from music\ironic (1950s swing, ragtime, naive sweetness, elevator muzak — run python get_music.py once to fetch them, credits handled automatically), plays it straight and a notch louder than a normal bed, then tape-stops on the reveal beat's first word — a quick slurring pitch-drop, a soft impact (the riser is skipped so nothing telegraphs the dread early), a beat of silence, and the song resuming slowed and quiet, like it's still playing from another room. The command line offers --ironic-music stop to end in silence instead. Works with any visuals mode and any category. And when 🎭 Mood is on 🎯 Auto, checking the box pairs the song with 🌤️ Wholesome visuals automatically — soft warm light and gentle smiles rendered as sincerely as the music while the script goes wrong, the full audio-visual contradiction in one click (pick an explicit mood to override the look; the script's register isn't touched).
  • 🎬 Movie-trailer feel (a Render checkbox — the two music treatments exclude each other): an epic cinematic bed from music\trailer (percussion hits, swelling strings — python get_music.py fetches them, credits handled) with the riser + impact landing on the reveal beat in every category, not just Scary Story. With 🎭 Mood on Auto, generated visuals render in 🎞️ Trailer mood — anamorphic teal-and-orange, dramatic rim light. There are now two trailer registers in the 🎭 Mood dropdown: 🎞️ Trailer — dialogue only (the default modern school: no narrator at all — every beat is 1–2 short character lines trading against the sound design, the cold-open hook is a character line too, and nobody speaks over the closing card; a beat can also be exactly (silence) — a held wordless shot where only the sound design plays, and the writer drops 1–2 of these in at tension points. Type (silence) into any spoken line yourself to hold a shot. For a real story arc, use 12–15 clips — and you don't need to re-draft for that: with a trailer mood selected, 📝 Script from prompts grows the script to the Clips count picked in step 1, re-telling the story across more scenes (the new beats get fresh prompts — tweak them or hit 🎬 Prompts from script after)) and 🎙 Trailer — narrated (the classic VO: cut-card fragments with 2–3 dialogue lines woven in). Pick one and hit 📝 Script from prompts. In trailer renders, dialogue lines get extra breathing room between them so the music and sound design can live in the gaps. Every trailer now carries a real score from music\trailer, mixed at soundtrack level — nearly three times louder than a narration bed, so the music carries the drama (and buries any synthetic edge in the voices). It pumps like a real trailer: while a character speaks it dips smoothly to ~30% so every word stays clear, then swells back the moment the line ends, and the whole score grows into the reveal. The score fits the story: four genres — ⚔️ Action (percussion, battle strings), 🌑 Dark (brooding tension), 💔 Tragedy (emotional strings, the Titanic register), ✨ Wonder (awe and discovery) — and the writer picks one when it drafts a trailer script (saved in the package; the render infers it from the title + premise for older packages). A 🎼 Score dropdown appears next to the 🎬 checkbox to force a genre; --score does the same on the command line. Each genre ships with 4–6 tracks, and you can add your own right from the page: pick a genre in the 🎼 dropdown, hit ⬆ Add tracks, choose your audio files, and (if the license requires it) paste the attribution line when asked — the tracks land in pipeline\music\trailer\<genre>, the credit goes into music\credits.json, and every post kit that uses the track carries it automatically. Dropping files into those folders by hand works too. Every score is loudness-normalized, so anything from a quiet piano piece to an epic battle track lands at the same level. YouTube's Audio Library (studio.youtube.com) is a safe source — but don't rip audio out of regular YouTube videos; downloadable isn't licensed. See pipeline\music\README.md. For horror packages the score is layered with a synthesized dread bed — heartbeat pulse, detuned drone, sparse metallic shrieks, the American Horror Story intro school, building to a climax on the reveal — generated from scratch, so there's nothing to license (--trailer-bed epic = score only, --trailer-bed dread = bed only). And every scene cut lands a stinger — a low boom, each varying slightly in weight and pitch — so the edit punches instead of drifting between shots; the reveal keeps its own riser + impact, and --no-sfx turns all of it off. The 🎢 Happy open → dark turn checkbox adds the modern trailer arc: a cheerful song opens the video bright (one of 🎵 My tracks if selected, else the ironic shelf), then around the middle it tape-stops on a heavy boom while a huge word card punches on screen in sync (the writer invents the word per story — "IT LEARNED" — and drafts open their first beats warm so the turn lands), and the dark genre score + dread bed take over through the reveal. And 🎵 My tracks is your own song library: pick it in the 🎼 dropdown, hit ⬆ Add tracks to upload songs you made or licensed (they land in music\custom), then select a specific song — it becomes the happy opener when 🎢 is on, or the whole score otherwise, loudness-normalized like everything else.
  • 🎙 Characters talk — any spoken line can embed in-scene dialogue in the form [Name] "the line" (the 🎞️ Trailer script writer adds these itself; you can also type them into any beat's spoken line or the ✏️ Script box). At render time each named character gets their own voice — cast automatically from distinct free edge-tts voices (or your ElevenLabs account's voices when that's on), with a subtle in-scene room tone so they sound like they're in the world, not in the booth. The narrator keeps your chosen voice, the tag itself is never spoken, and the render log prints the cast (e.g. Mara → Aria, Sheriff → Guy). On screen, dialogue captions are speaker-aware: each character's words render in italics with their own tint (ice blue, rose, mint — by order of appearance), a small — NAME flashes above the captions when they start speaking, and the spoken-word pop stays yellow for everyone. And with ElevenLabs on, dialogue lines act: character lines render on ElevenLabs v3 (the expressive model) and can open with an emotion cue inside the quotes — [Mara] "[whispers] It knows my name." — which the voice performs ([whispers], [terrified], [angry], [sighs], [shouting]…). The trailer writers add these cues themselves; you can type them into any line. Cues are direction, not words: they're never spoken, never captioned, and every non-v3 voice path strips them safely. Note: the AI clips don't lip-sync — trailer-style quick cuts carry it, so prefer wide shots or reaction shots on dialogue beats.
  • 🧬 Characters keep their look. New drafts (the batch buttons, 💡 Draft it) and 📝 Script from prompts now write a cast into the package — each recurring character's name plus a short fixed visual description ("Mara — mid-30s, short dark hair, red parka"). Because every AI clip is generated independently, the pipeline pins that exact look into every prompt that mentions the character: the OpenAI polish is told to describe them word-for-word the same, the free path expands the first mention ("Mara" → "Mara (mid-30s, short dark hair, red parka)"), and 🎬 Prompts from script carries the cast too — so the same person walks through all seven shots instead of a new face per clip. It's prompt-level consistency, not a face lock — expect strong resemblance, not identity; keep looks distinctive (one signature clothing item does most of the work).
  • 🎬 If a movie was AI-generated — the AI-remake format: pick it in the 💡 idea box's kind dropdown, type just a movie name ("Saw", "The Blair Witch Project" — optionally an angle after a dash), and Draft it writes an original "If <Movie> Was AI-Generated" dialogue-only trailer: brand-new lines in the film's vibe (never actual quotes — that's enforced in the writer's instructions), role names over trademarked character names, leaning slightly into the uncanny "an AI dreamed this movie" feel. Gets its own branding: electric-violet titles, epic trailer bed, reveal sound design, #aitrailer post kits, and "Every frame is AI" as the extra caption — the format is the AI disclosure. Heads-up on picking targets: naming a film for commentary is standard practice, but big-studio IP (Disney especially) draws takedowns on monetized posts more than horror franchises do.
  • 🗣️ …in AI language — the same movie-riff format, but every spoken line is an invented pseudo-language: words that sound like English (Simlish-style), Chinese, Spanish, French, Japanese, German, Tagalog, or Vietnamese, but mean nothing. Type The Lion King - chinese (language after the dash; default is AI English). The voices pronounce the fake words, lips sync to them, and the word-synced captions turn into gibberish karaoke — which is half the joke. Names, emotion cues, prompts, and captions framing stay in real English. Bonus: this is the most copyright-proof variant — there's no dialogue to compare to anything.
  • Selecting a package pre-fills the 💡 idea box — movie name, title, kind, and AI language are read from the package's own title, so a variant (same movie, another language) is two clicks: pick the package, change the language, 💡 Draft it. Your own typing is never overwritten, and changing the kind or language clears the now-stale title so the writer invents the right one at draft time.
  • 💡 Draft from your own idea (top of Produce, under the two draft buttons): paste a summary or a few details — a premise, a news story, something that happened to you — pick the kind (😱 Scary story / 💭 Realistic what-if / 📜 True history), and 💡 Draft it builds the whole package. No idea handy? ✨ Invent one has the AI write the idea too — a fresh suggestion for the selected kind lands in the box (click again for another), you tweak it, then Draft it: an invented title (or type your own), the full script, and per-beat shot prompts, honoring the Clips count and the 🎭 Mood. The writer keeps every named fact, person, and place from your notes. The new package opens below automatically, ready to tweak and render.
  • Radio on the video: that beat plays your uploaded clip as-is — in every mode, including ⚡ AI video, where it also skips that beat's generation cost entirely. Mix freely: some beats from images, some from your own clips, the rest AI-generated. Add Keep clip sound at render time if your clip's audio should stay under the voiceover.
  • Re-renders never overwrite. Render the same package again (new script, different clips, another voice…) and the previous video stays put — the new one saves as -v2, -v3, … with its own post kit and thumbnail, all side by side on the Videos page. Delete the takes you don't want from there.
  • Tick ElevenLabs voice, pick a narrator from the Voice dropdown — click ▶ Hear it to play a short sample of any voice (free) before you commit. Auto shows who the package's style would pick. Found a narrator you love? ☆ Make default pins it (★) to the top of the list and preselects it for every future render; click again on your default to unpin and go back to Auto. Favorites stay pinned even after the default changes. Add Keep clip sound if you used LTX → Render → go do something else: when it finishes you get a desktop notification, a chime, and the tab title flips to ✅ DONE (allow notifications when the browser asks, the first time you render).

notes autosave…

Click again to unmark. Each card also shows the date the video was rendered next to its file size (hover it for the exact time). Use the view switcher (top right: 🔳 Cards / ▦ Compact / ☰ List — List is the default) to see fewer or more videos at once — Cards keeps notes and post kits in view, Compact fits more tiles per row, List is one row per video for quick reordering and marking; your choice is remembered. The sort dropdown next to it reorders the display (Newest, Oldest, Unposted first, Title) — it never touches your saved posting order, and the ↑↓ arrows come back when you return to “My order”.

  • ↑ ↓ set your posting order (it's saved). #1 = post next.

  • Sidebar → 💰 Spend tracks every metered API charge: tryinfer clip/image prices are the provider's exact billed amounts; OpenAI drafts and prompt polish are token-count estimates (fractions of a cent). Flat subs (ElevenLabs, tryinfer Studio) aren't metered.
  • The OpenAI · this month card can show your real billed OpenAI cost: create an Admin key at platform.openai.com (Settings → Admin keys) and drop it in pipeline\openai_admin_key.txt. Your remaining balance is something OpenAI only shows in their own dashboard — the card links straight to it.

6Post it (the part only you can do)

⬆ ✓ Caption + hashtags from the post kit ✓ Cover image (-thumb.jpg) ✓ "AI-generated content" toggle ON

Everything you need to paste is in the video's post kit — posting takes about two minutes.

  • Watch the video once — you're the editor of record.
  • Upload the .mp4, set the matching -thumb.jpg as the cover. Covers now show the full video title (auto-wrapped, auto-sized), so scrollers know exactly what the video is about without reading the caption.
  • Open the post kit (on the video's card) → paste a caption + the music credit. Every kit now includes a cross-posting block with the right hashtags for TikTok, YT Shorts, and Reels — same video, swap the tags.
  • Turn on the platform's AI-generated content disclosure → post.

7Log the numbers — the Results page

The feed is the only reviewer that counts. Results turns your posts into the data that decides what to make next.

  • After you post, click Mark uploaded on the video's card (Videos page) — that's what puts it on the 📊 Results page. The Videos page opens in the Fresh view by default: uploaded and hidden videos are skipped so you only see what still needs attention — switch the filter to All videos to see everything (hidden cards show dimmed). Each card also has a Hide button (next to Mark uploaded) to tuck away takes you're not posting without deleting them; it flips to Show to bring one back. Voice casting is now character-aware too: Dads get male voices, Moms female, Children actual child voices — inferred from each character's name and cast look.
  • Every few days, open Results and paste each video's views and ♥ likes per platform into its row (commas are fine; numbers save when you leave the box, and re-entering later just overwrites with the latest).
  • The rollups do the rest: total and average views by category AND by format — every render records its recipe (Classic, 🎵 Ironic cheerful, or 🎬 Movie trailer, plus the 🎭 mood and the visuals mode), each posted video wears its recipe as a badge, and "best category" / "best format" cards call the winners. A few posts in, "does the trailer format actually outperform?" answers itself. Videos rendered before format tagging count as Classic.
  • Everything is typed in by you and stays on your machine — nothing connects to TikTok, YouTube, or Instagram.

?When something's off

"Dashboard offline" or the AI writer won't work

The helper isn't running. Double-click Start-What-If-Studio.bat in the project folder, then try again.

"The writing service hiccuped"

The free AI writer handles one request at a time — wait a minute and click ✨ Write it for me again. To skip the line, paste an OpenAI API key into pipeline\openai_key.txt (it works on the very next click — no restart): drafts then come from OpenAI (a fraction of a cent each) and automatically fall back to the free writer if the key stops working.

I want different images for a video

Delete that scenario's folder inside pipeline\ai-visuals — the next render generates a fresh set (now with people in frame by default). For fresh prompt wording too, also delete the scenario's file in pipeline\polished-prompts.

My custom scenarios disappeared / "Memory only" badge

Custom scenarios save in this browser on this device. If the badge says Memory only, the browser is blocking storage for local files — export anything you want to keep, and use the same browser each time. (Your videos and exports are files on disk and are never affected.)

Where do my videos live on disk?

pipeline\output — each video with its cover image (-thumb.jpg) and post kit (-post.txt). Removed videos are in pipeline\output\trash.
